Nicko's Tacos

Description

Here we go. These aren't the best tacos you've ever had. But if you're hungry, they'll make you stop being hungry.
I taught myself this recipe through sheer will and the desire to eat tacos that taste just like they were made using
a packet of Ortega, without any of the hassle of having to buy 3 or 4 packets of Ortega each week.

I recommend putting as little thought into these as possible. That defeats the purpose of this totally non-traditional
abomination of a well loved dish. You're gonna want to throw the meat in the pan with some chopped onions and just let
it all cook while you watch your stories. Have you seen the new Netflix show about the weird priest on the fishing island?
Good stuff. Three snaps in a Z formation.

When your beef is done browning, just throw the rest of the ingredients in the pan and turn up the burner to medium high.
When that bad boy starts bubbling turn it down to a "grandma's simmer" where there is a nice slow bubbliness to it. After
20 minutes you should have a pan full of Cali-Mexi-Texi-lower Missouri inspired "taco" meat. Add it to your favorite kind
of tortilla and enjoy. I know I will.

Here's What You'll Need:

  • 1lb Ground Beef
  • 1 Cup Water
  • 1 Medium Onion
  • 1 Butt Ton of Cumin
  • 1 Bucket of Chili Seasoning
  • 1 Quarter Bucket of Oregeno
  • All the Garlic
  • Salt N' Peppa to taste
  • A Sprig of Parsley (For Decoration)

Steps

  1. Chop your onion and add it to the pan. Cook until soft, about 3 mins.
  2. Add ground beef and cook until brown, about 7 to 8 mins.
  3. Throw your seasonings in there. Don't be afraid to more! Do it!
  4. Add the water and bring to a boil. As soon as boil starts turn down to medium low
  5. When all the excess water is gone they're ready to eat!
  6. Scoop into your favorite form of pressed corn or flour, and add cheese
  7. Garnish with parsley and one of those plastic limes from the store.(Optional)
