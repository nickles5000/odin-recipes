Bologna Cake

Description

Some of my fondest memories involve hot summer days in Arkansas sitting on a porch and enjoying some ice cold bologna
cake. The sweetness from the icing and the salty greasiness of the bologna create a flavor combination that would make
Thomas Keller roll over in his grave. What? Chef Keller isn't dead? Now he is.

I know you'll be tempted to skip out on the olives on this one as they only appear to be part of the decoration but they
they are, in fact, a key ingredient. The brine adds a little joie de vivre to the dish that would certainly be missed if
they were to be omitted. If you don't have any olives it would be worth your while to have little Johnny pick some up when
you send him to the market to get your Pall Malls.

I've posted my grandma's recipe here just the way she used to make it. We would always enjoy a slice (or ten) of this delicious
cake after one of here famous dinners featuring Pineapple Meat Stick and Mayonnaise Salad.

Here's What You'll Need:

  • 1 Package of bologna
  • 12 oz cream chees (room temperature of course)
  • 3 tablespoons of onion powder
  • Cheese Whiz and olives for decoration

Steps

  1. In a large bowl combine the cream chesse and onion powder
  2. Mix with a spatula
  3. Take a slice of bologna and pat dry. Place on a olate
  4. Spread a layer of cream cheese mixture over the top
  5. Place another slice of bologna over that
  6. When you are out of bologna spread remaining cream cheese abomination around the sides
  7. Top with cream cheese and olives (Optional)
